# Privacy Policy

**Last Updated: February 4, 2025**

This Privacy Policy describes how **Discipleship Pathway** (“we,” “us,” or “our”) collects, uses, and shares information about you when you use our websites, applications, and related services (collectively, the “Services”).

---

## 1. Information We Collect

1. **Personal Information You Provide**  
   - **Google Account Information**: When you sign in using Google OAuth, we may collect your name, email address, and profile picture from your Google account.  
   - **Other Information**: If you provide additional information (e.g., through forms, profile settings, or support requests), we collect this information as well.

2. **Automatically Collected Information**  
   - **Log Data**: We may collect information such as your IP address, browser type, operating system, referring URLs, and date/time stamps.  
   - **Cookies and Similar Technologies**: We may use cookies or similar technologies to remember your preferences and authenticate your session.

---

## 2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for purposes such as:

- **Authentication**: To allow you to sign in securely through your Google account.  
- **Providing and Improving the Service**: To operate, maintain, and enhance the features of our Services.  
- **Communication**: To respond to inquiries or provide support if you contact us.  
- **Security and Fraud Prevention**: To protect our Services and users from potential threats or malicious activity.  
- **Legal Obligations**: To comply with legal and regulatory requirements.

## 3. How We Share Your Information

We do **not** sell or rent your personal information to third parties. We may share your information in the following circumstances:

- **Service Providers**: We may share information with vendors and service providers who need access to such information to perform services on our behalf (e.g., cloud hosting, analytics).  
- **Legal Requirements**: We may disclose your information when required by law, in response to legal process, or to protect our rights or the safety of our users.  
- **Consent**: We may share or disclose your information with your consent.

## 4. Cookies and Similar Technologies

We use cookies or similar technologies to:

- Remember your session and login status.  
- Track usage of our Services to improve user experience.  
- Conduct analytics.

Most browsers allow you to block or delete cookies. However, if you do so, some features of the Services may not function properly.
